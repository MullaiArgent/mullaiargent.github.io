Getting started

How the app is organised

RasidhuPOS is a shop counter you run from one window. A tab bar runs down the left; the tab you pick fills the rest of the screen. The bar along the bottom shows sync status, the clock, the Quick Report chip and a support link.

The tabs

TabWhat it is for
PurchaseRecord stock you buy from suppliers.
StocksEnter and read each day's closing stock and wastage.
BillingRing up sales and print or share bills.
InvoicingDesign your receipt and A4 invoice layout.
ReportsSales, purchase, stock, profit and other analytics.
Amount TallyClose out the day's cash and reconcile it.
Rate ChangeChange item sale prices, with a change history.
ExpensesRecord shop spending by category.
EmployeesStaff records, salary and payments.
Audit LogA timeline of who changed what, with restore.

Tip. You can rename tabs, hide ones you do not use, and set your own keyboard shortcuts under Settings › Appearance. Because the shortcuts are yours to change, this guide points you to buttons and menus rather than fixed keys.

Getting started

Signing in

Each person has their own login. The owner account (Super Admin) can see every shop and every setting; cashier and admin accounts are scoped to their shop and to the tabs the owner allows.

  1. Start RasidhuPOS. On the login screen, pick your account.
  2. Type your password (or PIN, if your account uses one) and confirm.
Protecting the owner account. The owner (Super Admin) account can be secured with two-factor sign-in using Google Authenticator, which guards sensitive actions such as reinitializing the app. See Two-factor sign-in.

Selling & buying

Billing (make a sale)

The Billing tab is where you ring up a sale, enter the customer and item lines, take payment, and save the bill. Saving prints and/or saves a bill image, and can share it on WhatsApp or email.

Where: the Billing tab

The screen, top to bottom

  • Action bar: the auto bill number badge, and the New bill, Edit bill, Reprint bill and Cancel bill buttons, plus the full-screen toggle.
  • Customer card: the "Customer" and "Wholesale" choice, a large live total (Customer mode), and Name / Phone / Address / Date, plus a "More" button for email, GST state, GSTIN and an invoice description.
  • Ledger strip (Wholesale only): Previous balance, This bill, They paid and Closing balance.
  • Bill items: the entry row (Item #, Item name, Quantity, Rate, Amount, and "+ Add"), the line table, and a footer with Delivery, Discount, payment fields, "Print bill" and "Save bill".
  • Recent bills: a date-range list of recent bills you can copy, view, reprint or share.

How to make a walk-in (Customer) bill

  1. Leave the Customer option selected (it is the default).
  2. Optionally fill Name, Phone or Address. Filling any of these ticks "Delivery" automatically.
  3. In Item #, type an item code or name and select it (or pick from the dropdown). The name and rate fill in.
  4. Enter the Quantity, then the Amount if you want to bill by rupee value, and click + Add to add the line.
  5. Repeat for each item. Watch the large total in the top right.
  6. Optionally tick Discount (owner password) and type the rupee amount off.
  7. Click Save bill.

How to make a Wholesale bill (with a running account)

  1. Choose the Wholesale option.
  2. Pick the customer from the Wholesale dropdown. The phone fills in for you.
  3. Add items the same way. Here you type the Rate freely (no owner password needed).
  4. Enter what they paid in the cash Amount and/or UPI field. The ledger strip updates Previous balance, This bill, They paid and Closing balance live.
  5. Save. Any unpaid amount stays on the customer's running balance (khata).
Payment only, no items? To record a wholesale customer paying down their balance, pick them, add no lines, type the payment, and save. It is stored as a settlement.

Editing, cancelling and restoring

  • Edit a bill: click Edit bill (owner password), type the number and pick the issue date, then Open bill. The bill keeps its number when you save.
  • Cancel a bill: click Cancel bill (owner password), type the number and date, confirm. It is a soft delete: the bill stays in the records (shown struck through) for audit.
  • Restore a cancelled bill: right-click the struck-through row in Recent bills and choose "Restore this bill". It also restores from the Audit Log.
  • Reprint or share: right-click a recent bill row for View, Copy bill image, Send via WhatsApp, Send by email, Reprint or Edit.

Key controls

ControlWhat it does
Item #Accepts both a number and a name. Type "1" to match code 1, or "chi" to match Chicken items, then select it.
RateFor a customer sale the saved rate is used; changing it needs the owner password. For wholesale you type it freely.
AmountTyping an amount back-solves the quantity (amount divided by rate).
DeliveryMarks the bill a delivery. Tick "Send to WhatsApp" to auto-send the bill image on save (needs a phone).
Print billPrints the receipt on save. It is off and disabled until a receipt printer is set in Settings. Untick it to save without printing.
Save billSaves the bill, prints it (if Print bill is ticked), and shares it if configured.
Fast at the counter. The whole sale can be done from the keyboard: item, quantity, amount, add, repeat, then save. Bill numbers are assigned automatically and run as separate series for walk-in and wholesale.

Selling & buying

Purchase (record stock)

The Purchase tab records stock you buy from suppliers: items, quantities, per-unit rates and payment. It mirrors Billing, and keeps a running supplier ledger so you always know what you owe each supplier.

Where: the Purchase tab

How to record a purchase

  1. Pick the supplier from the Supplier dropdown. (Add suppliers in Settings › Suppliers.)
  2. Optionally change the Date (or click "Today") and type a note such as the supplier's bill number.
  3. In Item #, type a code or name and select it.
  4. Enter the quantity, then the per-unit rate (the faint hint is the last rate you paid this supplier for that item), and click + Add to add the line.
  5. Repeat for each item. Enter what you paid in the cash Amount and/or UPI field; the Closing ledger card updates live.
  6. Toggle Print bill, then click Save purchase.

The supplier ledger

The five cards across the top read: They owe us, We owe them, This bill, We paid and Closing. The closing balance is the previous balance plus what you paid, minus this bill. Green means they owe you; red means you owe them.

Editing, cancelling and restoring

  • Edit: "Edit purchase" (owner password), type the number and date, "Open purchase". Or double-click a row in Recent purchases and choose "Edit (owner)".
  • Cancel: "Cancel purchase" (owner password), type the number and date, confirm. Soft delete, kept for audit.
  • Restore: right-click the struck-through row in Recent purchases and choose "Restore this purchase".
Note. Purchase receipts print on the thermal printer only (there is no A4 invoice or email for purchases). Everything else works just like Billing.

Selling & buying

Rate Change (sale prices)

Change your items' selling prices in one place, with every change logged. New prices apply immediately to new bills and to stock valuation. Already-saved bills are never changed.

Where: the Rate Change tab

How to change an item's sale rate

  1. Open the Rate Change tab.
  2. Double-click the item's Current sale ₹ cell (owner and admin view) or Rate ₹ cell (cashier view).
  3. Type the new price and confirm the edit.
  4. Enter the admin password when prompted. The cell updates and the change appears in the history pane on the right.
Tip. Drag the handle at the start of a row to reorder your item list; that order carries over to Billing and Purchase. Cashiers get a simplified single-column view without the history pane.

Catalog

Items

Your product list. Items are looked up by their Item # in Billing and Purchase. Manage them in Settings.

Where: Settings › Catalog › Items

How to add an item

  1. Open Settings and go to Catalog › Items.
  2. Click + Add item.
  3. Give it a short Item # (a simple number like 1, 2, 3 is easiest to type at the till), a Name, and a Unit (for example kg or unit).
  4. Enter the Sale ₹ price and the Purchase ₹ (cost) price. Optionally set an opening stock.
  5. Click Apply or OK to save. The item is now available in Billing, Purchase, Stocks and Rate Change.
FieldWhat it means
#The item code you type in the Item # box while billing. Keep it short and unique.
NameThe item name, shown on the bill and searchable in the Item name box.
UnitThe selling unit, for example kg or unit.
Sale ₹The default selling price. Change it later from the Rate Change tab so the change is logged.
Purchase ₹The cost price, used for profit and stock valuation.
Set HSN / GSTOptional tax details (HSN code and GST rate) for GST invoices.
Base × RatioFor a derived item (for example a cut priced as a ratio of a base item), links it to its base.
Note. The selling rate is only changed from the Rate Change tab (so every change is logged); the Items page sets everything else.

Catalog

Wholesale customers & suppliers

Saved accounts you bill or buy from on a running balance. Walk-in customers do not need an account, you just type their name on the bill. Wholesale customers and suppliers are saved here so their ledger carries over.

Where: Settings › Catalog › Wholesale customers / Suppliers

How to add a wholesale customer or supplier

  1. Open Settings and go to Catalog.
  2. Pick Wholesale customers (for a shop or hotel you sell to on credit) or Suppliers (for a farm or wholesaler you buy from).
  3. Click Add, then fill the Name, Phone, Address and, if you keep an opening balance, the amount already owed.
  4. Save. The account now appears in the Wholesale dropdown in Billing (customers) or the Supplier dropdown in Purchase (suppliers), with its running ledger.

Daily operations

Stocks

A daily stock workbook. Enter each item's actual closing count for a chosen day; opening, purchased and sales reconcile automatically, and the day's stock loss (sinkage) is worked out for you.

Where: the Stocks tab

It follows one simple identity: opening + purchased = closing + sold + sinkage.

How to record closing stock

  1. Open the Stocks tab and set the Date.
  2. On the UNITS tab, click the Stock cell for an item and type the count left at the end of the day, then move to the next item. (You can instead type the units lost into the Sinkage cell and let Stock recompute.)
  3. Read the day's total loss and the biggest losses in the "Sinkage today" card. Switch to the AMOUNT tab for the rupee view.
Export. Use the PDF or Excel button at the top to export the active tab. To change an item's name or price, go to Settings › Items.

Daily operations

Expenses

Record shop spending (cash and/or UPI), organise it into colour-coded categories, and see per-day and per-category totals.

Where: the Expenses tab

How to record an expense

  1. Open the Expenses tab.
  2. Pick a Category.
  3. Type the cash amount in 💵 Amount ₹, the UPI amount in 📱 UPI ₹, or both.
  4. Set the Date if it is not today, and choose a Spread (leave "One-off (1 day)" for a normal same-day expense; longer spreads amortise the daily share).
  5. Optionally add a note in Notes / who paid, then click + Record.

How to add an expense category

  1. In the "Spending by category" pane on the right, click + Add.
  2. Type a Name (for example Rent) and pick a colour with Pick colour....
  3. Click Save. Use Rename, Recolor or Delete to manage existing categories.
Note. Filling both Amount and UPI records two rows (one per mode) so cash and UPI reporting stay exact. The "Salary" category is reserved for staff payouts from the Employees tab, and cannot be renamed or deleted. Editing or deleting a past expense needs the owner password.

Daily operations

Amount Tally (day close-out)

Reconcile a single day's cash at close: expected cash (Total A) versus what you counted and paid out (Total B). The banner tells you if the day is balanced, over or short. It is a cash-only view (UPI is tracked separately).

Where: the Amount Tally tab

How to close out a day

  1. Open the Amount Tally tab and set the date with the previous / next day arrows or the Today button.
  2. Count the cash and type it into Closing / Kalla and Cash in hand (use the + button to add to a value, the reset button to revert to the auto figure).
  3. Add any rows under Pending Amount and Due Clearance / Wholesale Payouts.
  4. Read the banner: CORRECT AMOUNT (balanced), EXCESS (over, green), or SHORTAGE (short, red).
Tip. Your counted closing cash carries forward as tomorrow's opening cash, so each day flows into the next. Wholesale cash bills appear automatically under "Due Clearance / Wholesale Payouts" so nothing is double counted.

Reports

Reports (Analytics)

Query, chart and export every kind of business report for any date range and any shop, plus a cross-shop Overview dashboard. Reports update automatically as you change the filters, there is no "Generate" button.

Where: the Reports tab

How to run a report

  1. Open the Reports tab.
  2. Choose a type in the Report dropdown.
  3. Set the dates with the Range presets (Today, 7 days, 30 days, 90 days, This year, Custom range) or the From / To pickers.
  4. Narrow further with Shop, Party, Mode or Item as offered. Click a column header to sort.
  5. Use ⤓ Export for PDF, Excel, CSV or "Copy as image", or ⧉ Undock to open the result in its own window beside another report.

The report types

  • Sales (bills), Purchases, Stock + wastage + valuation
  • Party ledger, Supplier ledger statement, Profit & Loss
  • Expense by category, Cash & UPI accounts, Day-end tally sheet
  • Weekly purchase by category, Items Sold (aggregate)
  • Item-wise sales (customer vs wholesale), Item-wise P/L, Item List / Rate Card
  • Employee salary, Employee ledger (detail)
Tip. Turn on 🔍 Search columns for a per-column search box, or ⚗ Filter for an advanced AND / OR query builder. On a Sales or Purchase report, click a row to see its line items in the detail pane, or use "Find bill by address / phone" to locate a specific bill. The Overview sub-tab shows a cross-shop dashboard with KPI cards and charts.

Invoicing & printing

Invoicing & bill design

Design exactly how your printed bill looks. A live preview shows your real layout; drag elements to move them, resize them, and edit the text. Separate layouts exist for the customer bill, the wholesale bill and the purchase bill.

Where: the Invoicing tab

How to design your bill

  1. Open the Invoicing tab.
  2. Pick the Invoice format (for example "88mm Thermal Receipt" or an A4 letter-pad), and tick Item prices already include GST (MRP-style) if that matches your pricing.
  3. Choose which layout to edit with the Customer Sale Bill, Wholesale Sale Bill or Purchase Bill tab.
  4. On the live preview: drag an element to move it; select it to change its size, font and style, or drag its handles to resize; double-click text to edit it; right-click for more options.
  5. Use + Add element to add fields or text. Click Test print to try it on your printer, then Save. Reset to defaults restores the standard layout.
Note. The second sub-tab, Notification Configuration, controls the WhatsApp and email messages sent with a bill. The customer layout also has a Walk-in and Delivery variant you can switch between while designing.

Account & security

Two-factor sign-in (2FA)

Pair Google Authenticator on your phone with the owner (Super Admin) account. Once paired, the 6-digit code from your phone is required to reinitialize the installation and to make changes to a Super Admin account. Until then those actions stay locked.

Where: Settings › Users & Security › Two-factor (2FA)

How to set up 2FA

  1. Install Google Authenticator (or any compatible authenticator app) on your phone.
  2. Open Settings and go to Users & Security › Two-factor (2FA).
  3. Click Set up Google Authenticator and scan the QR code (or type the shown key) into the app.
  4. Enter the current 6-digit code to confirm the pairing. The page then shows it as configured.
If you lose your phone, re-pair a new one on the same page using your Super Admin password. Use Remove... to unpair.

Account & security

Subscription

Your subscription is linked to your Google account and is valid for one year. The plan sets your caps: how many shops, how many bills and purchases per day, and how many staff accounts. It works on both the desktop and Android apps signed into the same Google account.

Where: Settings › About › Subscription

How to subscribe or renew

  1. Click Connect / switch Google account and sign in with the Google account you want the subscription tied to.
  2. Click Subscribe now, choose your plan, and pay by UPI.
  3. Submit your payment reference. Activation is manual and normally happens within one business day.
  4. Once activated, click Refresh now to pull the latest plan, caps and expiry into the app.
When a subscription expires, the app becomes read-only: you can still view all your data and it stays on your device and in your cloud backup, but you cannot add new bills until you renew. There is no lock-in.

Account & security

Cloud sync

RasidhuPOS is offline-first. While cloud sync is on, every change is also written to a Google Sheet on your signed-in Google account, so other shops or machines signed into the same account stay in sync. Everything keeps working offline; pending changes are queued and uploaded when the connection returns.

Where: Settings › Data & Sync › Cloud sync

How to turn on cloud sync

  1. Open Settings and go to Data & Sync › Cloud sync.
  2. Click Sign in with Google... and sign in.
  3. Tick Enable cloud sync.
  4. Press Sync now at any time to push and pull immediately (a short sync animation plays, which you can turn off on the same page).

Software updates

Keep the app current. RasidhuPOS can check for a newer version and apply it for you; your data is untouched by an update.

Account & security

Audit Log

A read-only, filterable timeline of who changed what, and from which shop. It is also where an admin can restore a deleted or cancelled record.

Where: the Audit Log tab

How to view and filter activity

  1. Open the Audit Log tab.
  2. Adjust the From / To dates and optionally pick a User, Action or Type. The table re-queries as you change a filter.
  3. Double-click any row to open the entry detail, with a before / after list of what changed.

How to restore a deleted or cancelled record

  1. Filter to find the row (Action is "Delete" or "Cancel").
  2. Click the blue Restore button in the last column.
  3. Confirm. The record is brought back and reappears in its tab, re-applying any stock and totals.
Note. The log keeps the latest 500 entries; narrow the date range to see older activity. Audit entries themselves are never edited or deleted from here.
